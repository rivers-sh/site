tech minimalism, and why it's important

(how to stop being addicted to your phone)

lets get one thing straight: tech minimalism does NOT mean being tech illiterate. tech minimalism is simply:

  • cutting out the noise (social media, time wasting apps)
  • using technology with intentionality (enhance productivity)
  • focusing on quality over quantity (focusing on what matters)

so, what can i do to convince you to practice this? well, nothing; it's much easier to over-indulge than to practice discipline and mindfulness. BUT, for those that are interested in practicing tech minimalism, i'll leave some advice below:

firstly, you should dump your smartphone and switch to a dumbphone, however, i realize the average person can't afford to just buy a new phone on a whim. a good alternative, is switching your phone to grayscale mode.

second, uninstall every social media app you have (i'm not telling you to quit social media) and only use them on your desktop or laptop- this will stop you from habitually pulling out your phone

lastly, if you still find yourself addicted to your phone, assign parental controls, and have one of your friends set up the password without telling you (this should be a last measure)
